The Huawei Ban



If you've been following the tech news lately then maybe you've come across the term "Huawei Ban" so let explain to you how things got this far, since May 2019 the Chinese tech giant was under attack by the United States government. Below, you’ll find all the detailed info related to the ban. We have also got some helpful tips related to Huawei’s smartphones and how the ban affects both current and future handsets.

First let's talk about Huawei and how it started. Relatively speaking, Huawei is a young company founded by Ren Zhengfei in 1987 after he was discharged from the People’s Liberation Army in China, His military history gave him a good advantage and helped Huawei get some of its first big contracts. This is one of the main reasons Huawei is viewed as a branch of the Chinese government.

Huawei has been accused many times of stealing intellectual property. in brief the company was accused several times of stealing technology from other companies and then passing it off as its own, However, there was only few times where this was proven, such as the case filed by Cisco in 2003, but there was many times where the accusations did not lead to confirmation. In the 2000's Huawei was growing at an unbelievable pace, the company started acquiring other companies to expand its operations. It at attempted to buy non chinese companies several times, however, each time the sale would be blocked, this happened in US and UK and other areas and the reason behind the block is the deep ties between the company itself and the chinese government and the security threat such sale would bring.

Eventually, Huawei started producing smartphones, and by the year 2016 Huawei claimed it would be the world’s largest smartphone manufacturer within five years, that was not a false claim, because by the 2018 Huawei had taken the second place ahead of Apple and just behind Samsung, even though it was slowed down by not having presence in the United States which is the world's third third largest market.

The Huawei ban begins


On May 15, 2019, an executive order was issued by Donald Trump that bans the use of telecommunications equipment from foreign firms for national security; the order itself didn't mention Huawei or even China, However, the US Department of Commerce made an Entity List related to the order that does contain Huawei's name. The order also didn’t make it clear whether the US government would help carriers pay for the removal of Huawei equipment. It also didn’t clarify any punishments US companies would face if they didn’t comply with the order. In brief, the Huawei ban seemed serious but there were too many unknowns to understand where it would go.

The Huawei Google ban


On May 15, 2019, Google declared that it would be complying with Trump's order, and they determined that the proper course of action would be to cut Huawei off from Google digital products pack. That means Huawei no longer has access to fundamentals of Android devices like Gmail, YouTube, Google Drive and even the Google Play Store. This news sent a shockwave through the tech world because at this point Huawei is the second-largest smartphone manufacturer globally, and every single one of its phones runs on Android.

When the dust settled, it became clear that Huawei phones certified by Google and launched prior to May 15, 2019, would continue to operate as normal. However, any uncertified phones, tablets, or other products released by Huawei after that date would be Google-less.

Not long after Google made its announcement, other US-based companies followed suit. This included Qualcomm, Intel, Arm, Microsoft, and many more.

Eventually, China tried to turn the tables by threatening to create its own Entity List. Huawei then upped the ante by accusing the US of cyberattacks and employee harassment. However, the company supplied no evidence to support these accusations and they led nowhere.

By mid-2020, Huawei had apparently accepted its fate. It stopped filing new lawsuits and stopped making any public declarations that it’s still trying to overturn the Huawei ban.

Somehow, Huawei continues being successful


You might think that throughout 2020 Huawei would have been struggling to stay afloat. However, Huawei actually made good on its promise and passed Samsung as the number one smartphone manufacturer as assessed by units shipped.

How is this possible? As mentioned before, you should never underestimate the power of 1.4 billion Chinese citizens all backing up their beloved homegrown brand. Also, don’t forget that Huawei doesn’t just make smartphones. It also still supplies networking systems to multiple countries all around the world.

Should you avoid buying Huawei phones or other products?


Well, it would be a bit of a different experience. You can do all usual stuff like pictures, surfing the web, gaming using social media. The only difference will be the apps you use to perform those functions will probably be different. For example, Google Chrome will not be available on new Huawei phones, so you’ll need to use a different browser. Huawei’s app store which is called App Gallery will have many of the apps you need.

Why can’t I just sideload Google apps?


You can sideload Android apps onto Huawei phones and a lot of them will work correctly. However, many prominent apps use something called Google Play Services to function. This Google product won’t be on new Huawei phones. There are several methods that have been used to successfully sideload Google Play Services on Huawei phones, but these are extremely unofficial, could potentially damage your phone, have no guarantee of working long-term, and potentially leave your device open to security risks. We do not recommend using this as a viable solution.
